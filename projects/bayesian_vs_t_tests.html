Bayesian vs Frequentist

  • Tech Stack: Python, A/B Testing, Statistics
  • Github URL: Project Link

Frequentist approach is time consuming where one have to wait to collect the sample size. Bayesian helps us remove that constraint. We can make data driven decisions in an efficient way.

We want to evaluate the clinical trial for a drug. The drug claim that it is suppose to increase intelligence by comparing IQ scores of individuals. Control recieves a placebo and Treatment recieves the drug

As we can see here, if we run a t-test on the same data, it gives a p-value of 0.12. According to industry standards, people are following 0.10 or 0.05 as a threshold. A statistican would have made a decision about the result being inconclusive based on the t-test.

While doing Bayesian Estimation, we observed that the difference in means is above 0 (97% of the time). This suggest that some significant difference exist between two groups. One can estimate that the drug have a significant effect on their IQ after consuming it.
